Core – Sprint 5

Final Reflections

Some new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning -

Through the emotional intelligence block I realised that I could really benefit from increasing my EQ for resilience purposes. Foundations has really pushed me much further than I ever thought would be possible, so having emotional intelligence in mind has been a helpful framework to learn and grow from.

Reflections on my why for studying at EDA have also been really eye opening on a personal level. I had a vision for my why before I started this course but it wasn’t fully realised, it was all hypothetical. I’ve had some great moments to reflect and feedback to myself much more than I realised was going to be possible. Feeling very thankful for the time and space to do process these kinds of ideas.

The role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming -

Values inform our personal strengths and weaknesses and in general tell us what makes us tick They play an important role in helping us to understand how and why we might respond to any challenges that come our way. New learning journeys are usually a constant challenge, so it's really important to know what you’re working with on a personal and professional level before you get started. It makes it easier to reflect and grow, and ultimately be kinder to yourself when you know your own limitations or boundaries.

Empathy in the same way plays an important role. If you can’t understand and share the feelings of another, you’re likely going to find it difficult to share your own feelings about the learning process and feel very isolated and alone in that reflection.

Self awareness is partially mentioned in both of the above points. Recognising personal emotions and how they affect thoughts and behaviour is very important when facing new challenges.

Surprises and the core learning -

Something that has taken me by surprise is that I thought I knew myself very well, and that was a fixed idea in my mind. What I hadn’t considered, and something that revealed it self to me in the core learning blocks is that there is always more to learn about ones self. I didn’t realise that I might not be as open as I thought I was to learning more about myself in order to grow into a more positive person.

Challenging aspects of the core learning –

I think self reflection is difficult but very essential! If I’m honest, sitting with information about myself that perhaps I don’t really like was challenging for me.

Why the focus on core learning in a web development bootcamp course -

Because we’re people and not computers! We have emotions, personal histories, values, dreams and so on. All of these things are just floating around in our minds at any given point in time, and don’t disappear while we’re all trying to apply ourselves to learning about web development. At some stage, something has to give if your brain is being overloaded. Core learning helps build resilience for that breaking point, and ultimately helps one help themselves.

Time spent on core learning vs programming -

Personally, I cannot learn technical information, or retain and apply it if my mind is already preoccupied with personal thoughts and stressors. I really value the balance of core learning vs programming. At some points I felt like I should just be focusing on programming, but in those exact moments, when I was putting a lot of pressure on myself, the space and time for self reflection was the exact right thing I needed to spend time on.
